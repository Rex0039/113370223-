[1. Research Question]
Does adolescent sleep duration significantly affect the frequency of physical fighting on school property?

[2. Variables]
- Independent Variable (X): Sleep Duration (Categorical: 4 hours or less to 10 hours or more)
- Dependent Variable (Y): Physical Fighting at School (Continuous/Ordinal frequency score)

[3. Methodology]
- Dataset: YRBS 2007
- Statistical Test: One-way ANOVA followed by Post-hoc Tukey HSD test.
- Valid Sample Size: 12040 observations.

[4. Key Findings & Results]
- F-Statistic: 67.7102
- P-Value: 3.37492e-83

Result Interpretation:
Since the p-value is less than the alpha level of 0.05, the result is STATISTICALLY SIGNIFICANT. 
We reject the null hypothesis. The data strongly indicates that different sleep durations have a significant impact on the frequency of physical fighting at school.

[5. Conclusion]
Adolescents with shorter sleep durations (especially those sleeping 4 hours or less) exhibit significantly higher frequencies of school violence. Promoting healthy sleep habits may serve as a potential intervention to improve school safety and reduce interpersonal physical fighting among high school students.
